ICCV 2025 Tutorial:

Foundation Models for 3D Asset Synthesis

Date

Time

Location

Overview

Diffusion models and autoregressive models have achieved groundbreaking progress in image and video generation, enabling the synthesis of high-fidelity content from text or image conditional inputs. However, extending these successes into 3D generation still faces significant challenges. Early optimization-based methods (e.g., DreamFusion) and reconstruction-based methods (e.g., LRM) laid the groundwork for this field, but often suffer from limited scalability, insufficient generation quality, or poor generalization. With further research, and inspired by the breakthroughs in image and video generation tasks, diffusion- and autoregressive-based methods for 3D generation have gradually emerged, opening new directions for 3D asset creation.

This tutorial will provide a systematic introduction to diffusion- and autoregressive-based 3D content generation, with a focus on data processing, algorithmic design, model training, and application prospects. Specifically, we will cover:

  1. Diffusion-based modeling for 3D geometry and texture generation
  2. Autoregressive paradigms for 3D generation
  3. 3D part generation
  4. 3D material generation

The goal of this tutorial is to bridge fundamental research in 3D generative modeling with practical applications in 3D asset creation. It is designed for a diverse audience:

  • Students and researchers: to gain a deeper understanding of the latest progress in 3D generation and identify open scientific challenges.
  • Industry practitioners and developers: to master key techniques and explore pathways for deployment in creative and production tools.
  • Creators and enthusiasts: to experience the potential of 3D generation in creative practices and enhance efficiency and expressiveness in asset design.

By consolidating current achievements and exploring future directions, this tutorial aims to help participants acquire a comprehensive understanding of the core techniques and challenges in 3D asset generation, providing inspiration for further academic research and industrial applications.

Yangguang Li

Diffusion Based 3D Assets Generation Foundation Models

Unlike image and video generation, progress in the 3D generation field has been relatively slow. Initially, optimization routes, represented by DreamFusion, were explored. This was followed by the exploration of reconstruction routes, such as LRM. It was only later that diffusion-based 3D generation techniques, similar to those in image and video generation, were gradually developed.

This topic will discuss the most advanced 3D asset generation solutions, including 3D data preprocessing, VAE reconstruction, diffusion models and texture generation.

Angela Dai

Can Transformers Speak Geometry? Autoregressive Mesh Generation

What if generating a 3D mesh was as simple as predicting the next word in a sentence? Autoregressive modeling has emerged as a unifying paradigm across modalities, ranging from text to images. We will explore its application to 3D geometry, with a focus on mesh generation using transformers.

By treating meshes as sequences, we can extend next-token prediction to mesh faces to tackle the compact and irregular structure of human-modeled meshes. We will discuss various design choices (e.g., in sequence formulation), as well as practical obstacles (e.g., scaling to high-resolution meshes, speed), with applications ranging from interactive design tools to automated prototyping and simulation-ready content creation.

Minghao Chen

From Whole to Parts: Part-Aware 3D Generation

Generative models have unlocked impressive 3D generation, yet most pipeline still treat objects as monolithic shapes. Real objects are compositional. Modeling at the part level brings controllability, editability, and functional reasoning to 3D generation at scale. This talk surveys the emerging landscape of part-aware methods and how they change data, training, and evaluation.

I will first cover our recent works, PartGen and AutoPartGen, which use multi-view diffusion and autoregressive modeling to generate structured 3D assets. I will then highlight key open problems and future directions, including unsupervised part discovery, structural consistency, and integration with large-scale 3D foundation models. By focusing on compositionality, this talk will outline how part-level generation could be a foundation for the next wave of 3D generative research and applications.

Zhaoxi Chen

From Synthetic to Physical: Material Synthesis for 3D Assets

Recent progress in generative 3D has made it easy to create shapes and scenes, but realistic materials—the appearance and behavior of surfaces under light and interaction—remain the bottleneck between compelling renderings and deployable digital/physical assets. We will first review material representations (BRDF/BSDF, SVBRDF, spatially varying microgeometry, and procedural graphs), differentiable rendering and inverse material capture, and the data/metrics landscape for perceptual and physical fidelity.

We then introduce synthetic material generation, 3DTopia-XL, which couples text/vision priors with scalable 3D material generation for editable, high-resolution assets; and PhysX-3D, which augments appearance models with physical constraints and affordance to align generated materials with real-world behavior. Future directions will be discussed by pushing materials from "looks right" to "acts right."
